Chicken Shawarma

What is Chicken Shawarma?

Chicken shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish that originated in the Levant region of the Arab world during the Ottoman Empire.

Ingredients

8 skinless chicken thighs (boneless)

1 large onion (thinly sliced)

1 large lemon (juice of)

⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil

¾ tablespoon ground cumin

¾ tablespoon turmeric powder

¾ tablespoon ground coriander

¾ tablespoon garlic powder

¾ tablespoon paprika

½ teaspoon ground cloves

½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (more if you prefer)

Salt

6 pita pockets

Greek tzatziki sauce (or tahini sauce)

Baby arugula

Mediterranean salad

Kalmata olives (or pickles, optional)

Instructions / How to Cook

1. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit.

2. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, turmeric, coriander, garlic powder, paprika, cloves, cayenne pepper, and salt.

3. Add the chicken and onions to the bowl and toss to coat.

4. Transfer the chicken and onions to a baking sheet and spread them out in a single layer.

5. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the onions are caramelized.

6. While the chicken is cooking, warm the pita pockets in the oven or on a stovetop griddle.

7. To assemble the shawarma, place a few pieces of chicken and onions in each pita pocket, along with some baby arugula, Mediterranean salad, and kalmata olives or pickles (if using).

8. Drizzle with tzatziki sauce or tahini sauce and serve immediately.

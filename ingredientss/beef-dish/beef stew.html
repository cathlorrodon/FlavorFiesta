Beef Stew Recipe

What is Beef Stew?

Beef Stews are made by simmering beef in liquid until it becomes tender. This process makes the beef tender while releasing all its natural flavor. The liquid can be water, beef stock or broth, or even wine. This recipe makes use of beef broth, which is readily available in any grocery store.

Ingredients

1/2 kilo beef cut into small cubes

1 can chicken sausage cut in to small slices

1 1/2 cups chopped celery ribs

3 medium potatoes cubed

1 1/2 teaspoons paprika

1/4 teaspoon McCormick ground cloves

2 pieces bay leaves dried or fresh

3 medium carrots chopped

2 medium red bell pepper chopped

260 g frozen green peas

1 tablespoon cornstarch diluted in 2 tbsp water

1 teaspoon garlic minced

1 medium onion chopped

2 cups beef broth

1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

2 teaspoon salt

2 tbsp Canola cooking oil

Instructions / How to Cook

1. In a large fry pan, brown the beef over a high heat for a couple of minutes.

2. Add onion, garlic, bay leaves, ground cloves, and paprika. Stir and cook for another 2 minutes.

3. Pour-in beef broth and let it boil.

4. Simmer for more than 1 hour or until beef becomes tender. Add water as needed.

5. Add carrots, potatoes, green peas, celery ribs, salt, and pepper. Stir and cook for 8 to 10 minutes.

6. Add red bell pepper, and pour-in diluted cornstarch then mix well and cook for another minute.

7. Taste and adjust seasoning, then remove from the heat and place to a serving bowl.

Beef Tenderloin Recipe

What is Beef Tenderloin?

The tenderloin is a lean cut with comparatively little intramuscular fat, known in culinary terms as marbling. And marbling happens to be one of the major factors in making a cut of beef moist and flavorful. Thus, beef tenderloin dries out if you overcook it. Moreover, despite its popularity, it's not known as a particularly flavorful cut of beef, which is why you'll often see a tenderloin steak prepped with a strip of bacon wrapped around it. The bacon adds flavor and moisture to a steak that otherwise might not have enough of either.

Ingredients

1/2 kilo Beef tenderloin tips

4 tablespoon of butter

1 (10 1/2) oz. can beef broth

1 bay leaf

2 pcs medium sized Bell Peppers, cut into strips

1 medium sized carrot, sliced

2 tbsp. peanut oil

1 pc. onion, chopped

2 tbsp. flour

1 can (400g) whole mushrooms, sliced

1/4 cup lemon juice

Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions / How to Cook

1. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in skillet.

2. Blend in flour and add beef broth stirring until smooth.

3. Add bay leaf and simmer for 2 or 3 minutes.

4. Remove bay leaf. In another pan melt 2 tablespoons butter.

5. Add onion and simmer for 3 or 4 minutes until tender.

6. Add mushrooms and continue to simmer until mushrooms begin to soften.

7. Add to broth mixture and then add lemon juice.

8. In another skillet heat 2 tablespoons peanut oil. Add beef tips.

9. Cook on medium high temperature for 2 or 3 minutes.

10. Then add carrots, bell pepper, salt and pepper.

11. Continue cooking until meat is lightly brown and bell peppers are tender. (4 or 5 minutes).

12. Do not over cook meat.

13. I use a large skillet for the meat and when it is browned add the broth mixture and stir gently until mixed.

14. This is good served with rice or noodles.
